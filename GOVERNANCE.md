## Project Model

Harbour Rust is an independent open source project maintained in public for community benefit. It is not a company product and does not operate as a commercial service.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- setting technical direction;
- reviewing and integrating contributions;
- protecting legal and editorial quality;
- keeping English and Portuguese public documentation aligned;
- documenting compatibility claims and known limits.

## Decision Making

- Prefer discussion through issues and pull requests.
- Prefer reversible decisions over broad speculative redesigns.
- When there is a tradeoff, compatibility, clarity, and maintainability take priority.
- The maintainer of record has final merge authority while the project is small.

## Documentation Governance

- English is the canonical language for public documentation.
- Portuguese (Brazil) is the official maintained translation.
- Structural changes to public documentation should update both language trees.
- Policy documents should not be copied from third parties without a clear legal basis.

## Community Position

The project welcomes international and Brazilian contributors. Technical disagreement is expected; disrespect is not. See [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Release Governance

- alpha releases may ship with documented compatibility gaps;
- every release must point to a written compatibility baseline;
- release notes should identify known limits and non-goals clearly.
